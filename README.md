## What This Is

This is a concept demo I built to explore what AI-powered internal tools could look like at Garner Health. Each tab represents a different department and persona — Finance, Legal, People, Talent, Sales, and Claims Analytics — and shows how Garner's unique data assets (60B+ claims, 500+ quality metrics, 700+ employer accounts) could be unlocked for the people who work there every day, not just for members.

The tools are UI-only mockups, but the product thinking behind them is real. Every tab is grounded in a job-to-be-done I've personally shipped against at some point in my career.

---

## Why Garner, Why Now

Garner has a rare combination that most companies never achieve: a defensible data moat, a mission that actually matters, and a business model that aligns incentives between employers, employees, and providers. The 60B+ claims corpus isn't just a marketing stat — it's the foundation for a class of AI products that no competitor can replicate from scratch.

What I find most compelling is that the hard part is already done. The data exists. The trust from employers exists. The question now is how to compound that advantage by turning it into intelligent, automated workflows for the teams building and operating the business — Legal, Finance, People, Talent, Sales, and beyond. That's the product surface I wanted to explore here.

## How My Background Maps to Each Tool

### Executive Topline · *Finance & Leadership*

At Verizon, I owned a $100M+ data and analytics transformation program that served the global finance and supply chain organizations. I built out integrated planning and reporting with Oracle EPM, processed 7.5 billion records with less than 0.01% variance, and delivered 200+ reusable data products across 16 core business dimensions. I know what it costs — in time, in headcount, in frustration — to produce a board-ready financial summary. The Executive Topline tool is my answer to that: NL-to-SQL over Garner's revenue and account data, combined with an AI narrative layer, so leadership can get to insight in minutes rather than waiting for a finance analyst to build a one-off report.

### Claims Analyst · *Data Science & ML Platform*

At AB InBev's Z-Tech innovation group, I served as Global Head of Data & Analytics and built out the ML platform from the ground up — feature store, automated training and inference pipelines, observability, and real-time serving for 150+ models. I also shipped an AI-powered credit score across LATAM that drove a 24% revenue increase in a six-month pilot. That experience gave me a deep appreciation for what it takes to turn a large, messy data corpus into trustworthy, production-grade AI decisions. Garner's 60B+ claims dataset is structurally similar: rich, proprietary, and massively underutilized as an internal analytical asset. The Claims Analyst tool is about giving data and product teams natural language access to that corpus — without writing SQL or waiting on data engineering.

### Legal & Compliance · *Responsible AI & Governance*

At Dayforce, I served as the product lead for a successful ISO 42001 audit — the international standard for AI management systems. I partnered directly with legal, security, and data governance teams to operationalize responsible AI practices across global deployments. That work taught me how to translate compliance requirements into product constraints and turn governance into a feature rather than a tax. The Legal & Compliance tool here is built around that same instinct: giving legal teams AI-assisted contract review, policy Q&A, and regulatory gap analysis that's fast enough to actually use, with the citations and auditability that legal teams demand.

### People & HR · *HCM AI Agents*

Dayforce is an HCM platform — the full stack of workforce management, talent, payroll, and benefits. My current role involves building the AI Agents layer across all of those domains: natural language interfaces that let employees and managers get answers, take actions, and automate workflows without navigating complex enterprise software. I shipped production-grade agents to a global customer base and drove 57% user adoption. The People & HR tool in this demo is a direct application of that work: an AI assistant for HR that can answer policy questions, surface workforce analytics, and handle common employee requests — the kind of thing that currently generates thousands of helpdesk tickets a quarter.

### Talent & Recruiting · *Bias Detection & JD Optimization*

Before Dayforce, I co-founded a company through the Barclays Techstars 2018 program that built a bias-detection tool for professional writing. I led the data scientists who built the core models and drove the product to first revenue. That company's core insight — that language patterns in job descriptions and performance reviews systematically disadvantage certain candidates — is even more relevant today as AI-generated content floods the hiring pipeline. The Talent tool here applies that same lens to Garner's recruiting workflows: flagging biased language in JDs, benchmarking compensation against market data, and giving recruiters an AI assistant that speeds up the hiring loop without introducing new sources of unfairness.

### Sales Intelligence · *GTM Collaboration*

At Dayforce, I worked in close partnership with go-to-market, sales, and executive leadership to set product vision, roadmap, positioning, and packaging for the GenAI platform. The result was the fastest-growing SKU with multi-million-dollar revenue impact — and a product that was featured in CTO and CPO keynote presentations. I understand how sales teams think about ROI, what they need to walk into a new employer meeting with confidence, and where the friction lives between "we have great data" and "the rep can actually use it." The Sales Intelligence tool is built around that friction: instant, account-specific ROI projections using Garner's claims and outcomes data, so every rep walks in prepared.

### Account Health · *Adoption Analytics & CSM Tooling*

At Dayforce, I tracked and drove 57% user adoption across a global customer base by analyzing usage data and running experiments against the product experience. I know what early warning signs of churn look like, and I know how hard it is for CSMs to surface those signals before it's too late. The Account Health tool is built around the same usage analytics infrastructure I worked with at Dayforce: an account-level dashboard that surfaces adoption gaps, engagement trends, and recommended interventions — so CSMs can be proactive rather than reactive.

### Score Workbench · *LLM Experimentation & Quality*

At Dayforce, I drove a 25% lift in user satisfaction by systematically analyzing LLM performance, running prompt strategy experiments, and closing feedback loops between model behavior and user outcomes. Garner's provider quality scores are the core product — they're what employers buy and what members rely on. The Score Workbench tool is about giving internal teams the ability to interrogate those scores: understand why a provider ranked where they did, compare scoring methodologies across specialties, and test how score changes would flow through to recommendations. It's the kind of internal tooling that makes the core product better over time.
